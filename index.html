About

I am a journalist based in Chicago. What drew me to journalism was my passion for learning about the world around me through stories. That passion has led me to seek out writing opportunities as near as Chicago and as far as the Dominican Republic. During my four years at Northwestern University, I've learned the ins and outs of reporting on business, the tech industry, public education and social justice. I'm eager to lea rn even more.

Above, you'll see links to the stories from different parts of my journalism career. For a deeper overview of all the places I have worked, please refer to my resume or contact me via email.

Medill Justice Project

"A Mother's Care" is the story that confirmed my desire to go into investigative journalism. Centered around an alleged medical child abuse case in Oregon, the story opened a dialogue on a rarely researched topic. During my 3 months with MJP, my team and I spent hours combing through legal documents, emails and anything we could get our hands on. I was able to earn the trust of sources on both sides, leading to a well-reported, award-winning piece.

Dow Jones News Fund

Above is the most studious photo of me ever taken -- an accurate reflection of my one-week Dow Jones News Fund residency before a summer spent reporting on business. I spent the week balancing lessons on reporting on investments, earnings and other aspects of the economy. I also visited the New York Stock Exchange and other famous institutions for on-the-ground experience.

The Youth Project

The Youth Project was my gateway into social justice reporting. Over the past three years I have researched and reported on issues facing youth in and around Chicago for the publication.

Catalyst Chicago

Catalyst Chicago is a nonprofit journalism outlet dedicated to educational reform in Chicago's schools. I spent my three month Journalism Residency at Catalyst. I started out writing weekly news recaps for the website's Take 5 section. Covering the ever-eventful CPS, I was soon running around Chicago live-tweeting strikes, elections and more, resulting in longer feature pieces.

North By Northwestern

My time as NBN News Editor forced me to get well-written, accurate stories written fast. I was able to cover a range of events, from standard student government meetings to a pro-Bernie pop-up Vampire Weekend concert on campus. It was also an opportunity to develop my editing skills and eye for news worthy events.

SourceMedia

I was placed at SourceMedia through the Dow Jones News Fund. Over the course of ten weeks I was able to write for three SourceMedia publications: Employee Benefit News, Employee Benefit Adviser and Health Data Management. My skills as a business journalist grew tremendously and I found ways to make numbers-based stories fun and interesting to read.
