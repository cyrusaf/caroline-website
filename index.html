About

I am a journalist and student based in Evanston, IL. I realized early in my journalism career that what drew me most to the profession was the ability to learn more about the world with each story. That passion to soak up as much new information I could about the world around me has led me to work as near as Chicago and as far as the Dominican Republic. Over just four short years as a student, I've learned the ins and outs of business reporting, the tech industry, public education and social justice -- and I'm eager to learn more.

Above, you'll see links to the stories I've been most passionate about from different segemnts of my journalism career. For a deeper overview of all of the places I have worked, please refer to my resume or feel free to contact me via LinkedIn or email. I am always excited to hear from fellow media lovers to share ideas.

Medill Justice Project

"A Mother's Care" is the story that confirmed my desire to go into investigative journalism. Centered around an alleged medical child abuse case in Oregon, the story opened a dialogue on a little researched topic. During my 3 months with MJP, my team and I spent hours combing through legal documents, emails and anything we could get our hands on. More importantly, I was able to form social connections with sources that allowed me to build trust and empathy with those on all sides.

Dow Jones News Fund

Above you will see the most studious photo of me every taken -- an accurate reflection at my one-week Dow Jones News Fund residency before the fund sent us off to work in business journalism for the summer. I spent the week balancing lessons on reporting on investing, earnings and other aspects of the economy and real world experience trips to the New York Stock Exchange and other famous institutions.

The Youth Project

The Youth Project was my gateway into social justice reporting in Chicago. I've continued to work for them over the past two years, researching and reporting on the rights of minorities, LGBT and other traditionally marginalized youth.

Catalyst Chicago

Catalyst Chicago is a nonprofit journalism outlet dedicated to educational reform in Chicago's schools. During my first week there, I started out with a few news recaps for their weekly Take 5's. Not long after, the Chicago Teacher's Union was on strike, the CPAA elections were going through unprecendented drama and I found myself running around the city live-tweeting, interviewing and writing features on the always eventful CPS.

North By Northwestern

My time as NBN News Editor forced me to get quality stories out fast enough to beat teh competition, while ensuring that my pieces were accurate. I was able to cover a range of events, from standard student government meetings to ice-cream promising rock stars appearing on campus to appeal for Bernie Sanders.

SourceMedia

I was placed at SourceMedia through the Dow Jones News Fund, following my one-week residency. I was able to write for three seperate publications during my time there: Employee Benefit News, Employee Benefit Adviser and Health Data Management. My skills as a business journalist grew tremendously, and I was able to find ways to make seemingly dry stories about fun (especially in my EBA piece on pet insurance).
